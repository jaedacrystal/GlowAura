Ceramides

Ceramides function like the natural "glue" that binds our skin cells together, maintaining the skin's structure, smoothness, and hydration. Acting as essential lipids in the skin's outer layer, they form a protective barrier that locks in moisture while shielding against environmental aggressors such as pollution, irritants, and harsh weather conditions. This barrier plays a crucial role in skin health by preventing water loss and maintaining elasticity.

When ceramide levels are low, the skin barrier weakens, leading to dryness, itchiness, and a heightened sensitivity to irritants. By replenishing ceramides through skincare, products help restore the barrier, alleviate inflammation, and improve hydration. This makes ceramides a vital ingredient for dry, sensitive, or compromised skin.

(Rashid, 2024)

Storage

Store your ceramide creams and serums in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and humidity, which can break down the ingredients. Always make sure the container is tightly closed after use to prevent air from getting in, which can degrade the product. Don’t leave your products in places with fluctuating temperatures, like a bathroom or a car, as heat can cause the formula to break down faster.
